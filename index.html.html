PWR-Bru Integrated Marketing Campaign

ClientAG Barr (Irn-Bru)
Year2025
Duration2 months
RoleStrategy & Comms
01 Overview

Integrated marketing communications campaign for PWR-Bru, the energy variant of Irn-Bru under AG Barr. A two-month brief delivered as a unified plan across digital, outdoor and in-store channels, presented to industry stakeholders within AG Barr's marketing and commercial teams.

The work set out to reframe PWR-Bru for a younger, performance-led audience — pushing beyond the heritage Irn-Bru identity into a tighter, sharper energy-drink positioning without losing the parent brand's cultural equity in Scotland.

02 The Challenge

PWR-Bru sat inside one of the UK's most iconic brand houses, yet was losing ground to pure-play energy challengers like Monster and Red Bull. The task was to build a distinctive identity and communications system that carved out its own space in a saturated category, while still benefitting from the Irn-Bru lineage.

03 Approach
01
Audience & channel audit Mapped target consumer behaviour across digital, OOH and in-store touchpoints to identify highest-leverage moments.
02
Unified creative platform Developed a single campaign idea flexible enough to run across social, outdoor and retail activation, with consistent tone and visual system.
03
Performance-led KPI design Structured measurement around awareness, acquisition and retention, with channel-specific targets tied to the broader business case.
04
Stakeholder presentation Built a pitch deck and rationale document presented to AG Barr industry stakeholders.
04 Outcome

The plan was presented to AG Barr industry stakeholders with clear channel strategy, creative direction and measurement framework. The campaign was structured to deliver a 20% awareness uplift and 100,000 new followers within the activation window.

Brand Strategy · Repositioning

Tennent's Lager Brand Repositioning

ClientC&C Group
Year2025
Duration2 months
RoleBrand Strategy
01 Overview

Full brand audit and repositioning strategy for Tennent's Lager, Scotland's largest beer brand. A two-month strategic engagement with the brief evaluated by C&C Group senior management.

The work reframed a heritage lager for a new generation without abandoning its cultural roots — using research to surface a substantial untapped audience and proposing activation routes that could reach them authentically.

02 The Challenge

Tennent's carried enormous brand equity in Scotland but faced shrinking relevance with younger drinkers. The brand was ceding share to craft and premium lagers while its core audience aged out. The task: identify where growth could come from and design a repositioning credible enough to earn leadership buy-in.

03 Approach
01
Brand audit Assessed current positioning, visual identity, communications and distribution against historical equity and present-day performance.
02
Competitor positioning analysis Mapped the Scottish lager landscape — premium craft, imports, and regional peers — to locate white space.
03
Consumer segmentation Research-led segmentation revealed a 900,000-strong audience aged 18–35 underserved by the current brand expression.
04
Repositioning strategy Built across three pillars: influencer marketing, product expansion and brand activism — each with clear rationale and activation paths.
04 Outcome

Delivered as a board-level recommendation and evaluated by C&C Group senior management. The strategy gave Tennent's a defensible growth route targeting a 900,000-strong audience aged 18–35 — one the brand had not previously activated against.

05 Impact
900K Untapped audience identified
18–35 Target demographic

Rubicon Fru-T Product Launch Plan

ClientAG Barr (Rubicon)
Year2025
Duration2 months
RoleGTM Lead
01 Overview

Board-ready launch plan for Rubicon Fru-T, a Gen Z targeted sparkling iced green tea positioned under the Rubicon brand at AG Barr. The plan covered product, packaging, pricing and shopper marketing — evaluated by AG Barr's Commercial Director.

The engagement identified a gap in the ready-to-drink tea category where existing brands skewed older and more functional, and used it as the launchpad for a distinctly Gen Z sparkling iced tea concept.

02 The Challenge

The RTD tea category is crowded with wellness-led incumbents but thin on sparkling, youth-coded entries. AG Barr needed a launch plan that justified investment — with a defensible target, commercial sizing, and clear route to shelf — all within the Rubicon masterbrand architecture.

03 Approach
01
Category gap analysis Identified white space within RTD tea — sparkling, Gen Z-focused — against wellness-led incumbents and premium imports.
02
Product & packaging proposition Defined format, flavour architecture and pack design principles aligned with the Rubicon masterbrand and the Gen Z target.
03
Pricing & channel strategy Built a commercial model sized for £5.8M Year 1 RSP, with channel prioritisation across grocery, convenience and on-trade.
04
Shopper marketing & activation Designed an in-store and digital activation plan to drive trial at launch and sustain rate of sale post-launch.
04 Outcome

A complete launch plan — product, packaging, pricing, shopper — delivered and evaluated by AG Barr's Commercial Director, with a commercial case projecting £5.8M RSP in Year 1.
